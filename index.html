About

I am a first-year Ph.D. student in the School of Interactive Computing at Georgia Tech co-advised by Prof. Alan Ritter and Prof. Wei Xu. I received my M.S. in Computer Science from KAIST, where I worked with Prof. Sung-Hyon Myaeng.

My primary research interests lie in the area of natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning (ML). The long-term goal of my research is to enhance the practicality of NLP systems (e.g., Large Language Models) so that they can be widely used in real-world scenarios. In particular, my research focuses on i) how to make NLP models cheaper (i.e., efficient) in terms of data, parameters, or compute, and ii) how to build NLP models that are robust to unseen cases in the wild. Here are some keywords:

  • Large Language Models
  • Retrieval-Based NLP
  • Efficiency
  • Robustness
